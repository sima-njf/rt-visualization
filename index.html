🔬 Rt Estimation Interactive Grids

Publication-Quality 2×2 Grid Visualizations

Simulation Parameters

📊 Population

Size: 100,000
Initial infected: 100 (0.1%)
Simulations: 100 per scenario

🦠 Disease Model

Model: SEIR (Susceptible-Exposed-Infected-Recovered)
Incubation: 4 days
Recovery: 7 days (rate = 1/7)

🔗 Contact Structure

Full Model: Contact rate = 20
Partial Model: Erdős-Rényi network, avg degree = 10
Days simulated: 150

📈 R₀ Values Tested

Basic reproduction numbers:
R₀ = 1.5, 2.0, 3.0, 5.0
Transmission rate: Calculated as R₀ × recovery_rate / contact_rate

⏱️ Serial Intervals

Method: Calculated from transmission data
Definition: Time between symptom onset of infector and infectee
Used in: Both Cori and Wallinga-Teunis methods

📉 Transitions Tracked

S → E: Susceptible to Exposed
E → I: Exposed to Infected
Both transitions analyzed separately for Rt estimation

🎯 Real Data Scenario

Perfect reporting: 100% of cases reported
No delays: Instantaneous reporting
Baseline: Used to validate estimation methods

⏳ Delayed Scenarios

50% Delayed: Half of cases delayed by Uniform(1,7) days
Purpose: Test robustness to reporting delays

📊 70% Reporting

Underreporting: Only 70% of cases reported (binomial thinning)
Purpose: Test impact of incomplete surveillance
